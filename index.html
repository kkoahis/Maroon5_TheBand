BIOGRAPHY

Maroon 5

Maroon 5 is a band formed in Los Angeles, California, United States. Adam and Jesse, who were junior high school students at the time, started playing music together, and from 1994 to 2001 they were active as a four-piece band under the name Kara's Flowers, but when they added new members and became a five-piece band, they changed their name to Maroon 5.
His 2002 debut Songs About Jane became an astonishingly long hit album, winning him a Grammy Award for Best Newcomer in 2005. In Japan, two songs in a row, "Sea Will Be Loved" (Noevir CF) and "Sunday Morning" (Toyota Bits CF), were used in commercials in quick succession, becoming an unprecedented hit. At the 2006 Grammy Awards, he won Best Pop Performance (Duo/Group Vocal) for the single "This Love".

Released in 2007, It Won't Be Soon Before Long debuted at No. 1 on the U.S. Albums Chart, and their debut single "Make Me Wonder" won the 50th Grammy Award for Best Pop Performance (Duo/Group Vocal).
After the release of their third album, Hands All Over, vocalist Adam Levine was selected to be a judge for the hugely popular US program "The Voice." "Moves Like Jaguar" featuring Christina Aguilera, who was a judge at the same time, became a big hit, reaching No. 1 on the US Singles Chart for four consecutive weeks.

From the album "Overexposed" released in 2012, "Payphone" and many other hits were mass-produced. In 2013, Adam Levine was named "Sexiest Man in the World" by People magazine.
His 2015 album V also reached No. 1 on the U.S. Albums Chart. Adam was inducted into Hollywood's "Walk of Fame" Star Hall of Fame in February 2017.

In November 2017, they released their sixth album in about three years, Red Pill Blues. The worldwide hit single "Girls Like You" spent seven consecutive weeks at No. 1 on the US Billboard Singles Chart. The song reached No. 1 on iTunes and Spotify, with more than 1.2 billion views on Spotify and more than 3 billion views on YouTube/VEVO, making it the most watched video on VEVO in 2018.
In both name and reality, the band boasts the top class popularity not only in the United States but also in the world.
